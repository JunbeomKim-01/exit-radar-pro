Frequently Asked Questions

Form 4 is filed when corporate insiders trade stock. It provides transparency into executive and director buying or selling activity.

These are SEC filings required when an investor acquires 5% or more of a public company's shares. 13D is filed by active investors (such as activists), while 13G is filed by passive investors.

Form 13F is a quarterly filing by institutional investment managers disclosing stock holdings. We process 13F filings in real time.

Large or unusual insider buying patterns often indicate confidence and can inform investment research.

All filings are updated in real time as they are filed with the SEC.
